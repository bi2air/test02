## Compromise And Rationale

The compromise is to prioritize **clean controllable coverage first** over immediate real-production anchoring. Real conversations remain the long-term grounding source, but today's calibration showed that extracting clean single-intent examples from messy transcripts is not reliable enough to scale first. The safer path is to seed the sample database with controlled canonical examples, then use human review, selective regeneration, and later real-conversation augmentation to close realism gaps.

This is an acceptable compromise for KR1 because the near-term goal is balanced, reviewable coverage across the taxonomy. It is not a final truth set. The risk is that synthetic examples may be cleaner than production behavior; the control is to keep review notes, spot-check harder levels, and use real-conversation augmentation only where it adds value after the canonical seed is stable.

## Approach

1. Start from the OKR target, not from the available artifacts: KR1 needs balanced coverage; KR2 needs measurable reduction in manual QC effort.
2. Use calibration as the gate for real-conversation extraction; if the extractor cannot match reviewer judgement, do not scale it as the seed source.
3. Prefer controlled generation for canonical examples when the goal is to seed clean behavior, then use review to catch boundary contamination.
4. Separate sample-building work from Auto-QC work so coverage growth and classifier-quality diagnostics do not blur into one metric.
5. Treat fast-vs-final differences as a pipeline-stability question first, not as a direct classifier-quality score.
6. Require model-aligned validation before turning redaction or rendering conclusions into shared recommendations.
7. Keep failed or partial trials as reusable assets, but report them under the workstream they actually support.

## Data Selection And Alignment

- Selection gate: include examples because they cover a target code and difficulty level, not because they are easy to find.
- L1 gate: require one substantive intent; rows that combine multiple code signals should be reviewed or regenerated.
- Coverage gate: keep the seed set tied to the current call-code taxonomy and the quarter target for per-code balance.
- Production-record gate: use final stabilized labels for reporting; keep preliminary labels as diagnostic inputs.
- Model-alignment gate: do not publish rendering or redaction conclusions until checked against the production classifier setup.
- Review gate: treat voter disagreement and instability as routing signals for Auto-QC, not as failures to hide.
