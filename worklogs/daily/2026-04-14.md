## AGI-8632 · Compliance testset baseline — 63.5% accuracy

**Why.** **AGI-8632** ([C09] Call disposition codes audited & redial logic tested) is the umbrella ticket for the compliance-class disposition audit. Compliance-class call codes (DNC, CDS, DIS, ATY, FRA, HCH, NCV, WCR, WRN, DSA, CPR) need a separate accuracy baseline because they carry regulatory risk — different bar than operational codes (≥95% vs ≥90% per the compliance-vs-ops split). Today establishes that baseline using a fresh testset against the Apr 13 prompt — the first metric on AGI-8632 work.

> ### 🛠 What I did
> Built the compliance testset (per `plan_compliance_testset_0414.md`) and ran the classifier in two rounds (round 1 + round 2 + revised round 2). Designed the prompt variant `design_prompt_apr14_v1` and the canonical detection prompt `prompt_detect_call_code_apr14_v1.txt`. Ran the experiment captured in `compliance_exp_apr14.md` and follow-ups.

**Result.** **63.5% accuracy on generic compliance defs.** Per-code breakdown:
- HCH, NCV, DNC — need tuning (under-bar performance).
- WCR — labels mostly wrong (suggests label-quality issue, not just classifier issue).
- Other codes — at or above bar.

This becomes the seed for the Apr 15 R4 testset (120 rows, 3-run consensus labeling).
