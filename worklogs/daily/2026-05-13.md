## Approach

1. Start from accounted production-derived evidence before expanding the evaluation set.
2. Keep evaluation populations separate: high-confidence anchors, disagreement rows, compliance-sensitive rows, deterministic system rows, and rows that require human adjudication.
3. Require version and route alignment before using a row for current-quality reporting.
4. Treat reviewer disagreement as signal, not noise; use it to prioritize Auto-QC and seed-revival work.
5. Keep redaction visible in the review process so masked evidence is not over-interpreted when exact wording matters.

## Data Selection And Alignment

- Selection gate: choose rows because they test a specific signal, not just because they are available.
- Required-input gate: only rerun rows whose conversation evidence and state inputs are complete enough for the target evaluation layer.
- Configuration gate: do not mix current-route results with historical or mismatched configurations in the same headline metric.
- Review gate: route disagreements, sensitive evidence masking, and fast-vs-final instability into human review before treating them as high-confidence labels.
- Scale gate: expand only after the small experiment proves both the runner path and the human-review packet shape.
