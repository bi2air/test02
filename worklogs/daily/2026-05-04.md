## AGI-9466 · NNP / RTP language-register boundary

**Why.** **NNP (No Negotiation Possible)** and **RTP (Refuse to Pay)** were easily confused — both surface as "I won't pay" patterns. Without a clear **language-register** boundary, the classifier defaulted to RTP for soft refusals, missing the NNP signal. The two need different downstream actions: **NNP** suggests the customer is open to alternatives if approached differently; **RTP** is a hard wall that ends negotiation.

> ### 🛠 What I did
> Pinned the boundary criterion explicitly: **NNP = soft / neutral / non-committal language; RTP = hard / unambiguous; the count of declines does not determine the code** — a customer can decline 5 times softly (still NNP) or once hard ("I refuse" — RTP). Ran `paco-agi9466-v2@d43bc8e` against NNP × 3 cases on both classifier paths to validate.

**Result.** **3/3 exact match plus found-in for NNP × 3 in both NORMAL and BASIC classifier paths.** Boundary criterion validated end-to-end. Captured in `memory/project_nnp_rtp_boundary_may04.md`.

**Links.**
