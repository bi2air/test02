## DCI-322 · 10k-SID stratified sampler — first end-to-end stats run

**Why.** DCI-322 needs a 10k-SID sample for **cloud-cutover byte-compare validation** of the CSV3 score pipeline (run scoring on both on-prem and cloud, compare outputs SID-by-SID). The sampler design (Tier 0 stratified primaries × Tier 1 1st-hop neighbours) was specced Tue Apr 15 in `stratify_sampling.md`. Today is the first end-to-end Spark run on real data — needed to confirm fanout assumptions (how many 1st-hop neighbours per primary?) before locking the production allocation. Complicated by an **Apr 13 disposal-archival event** that moved working-prod data to `/data/backup_not_delete/de/disposal_2026_04_13/` — every parquet read needs a fallback path.

> ### 🛠 What I did
> - **Scaled trial: 1,000 primaries (4× the production 250)** to get tighter fanout statistics. Smaller sample = noisier per-bin counts.
> - **Activity-window filter validated** — `feat_activity` read at 5 checkpoints (T, T-8w, T-16w, T-24w, T-32w spanning May–Dec 2025); SIDs passing `activity_4w=1` at ≥3 of 5 checkpoints kept as candidate primaries. Candidate pool: **2,080,629 SIDs** distributed across 4 age bins (`lt6m=193,886 · 6_12m=186,843 · 12_36m=514,888 · gt36m=1,185,012`).
> - **1st-hop fanout** — read `idata/graphio_8w` at 5 lags `[0, 8, 16, 24, 32]` weeks (9-month coverage). Fanout: **mean=71.6, p50=10, p95=199, p99=1,248, max=5,281**. Long-tail confirmed; dominated by ~10 small-fanout primaries with a handful of super-connector outliers.
> - **Distinct contacts: 51,788 → 51,787 after removing primaries → 41,859 (80.8%) after `activity_4w=1` filter** at the scoring date. Active 1st-hop spans **50 sid_groups** (every sid_group represented), so the cloud sample mirrors that spread.
> - **Archive-fallback pattern** embedded in the sampler — every `_read_parquet()` first probes `/data/working/...`, falls back to `/data/backup_not_delete/de/disposal_2026_04_13/...` if the primary path is missing.

**Result.**
- **Production allocation confirmed**: 250 primaries × ~40 active hop1/primary ≈ 10k total. The 4×4 (age × csv3_score) allocation matrix from `sample_10k_sids.py:66-77` (weighted 140/55/30/25 across age bins, emphasising `gt36m × high-score`) is realistic against the candidate pool.
- **Archive-fallback pattern** validated — needed for every read after Apr 13 disposal. Lifted into a reusable `read_with_archive.py` (73L) the next day (Fri Apr 17 15:08).
- **7-hour wall-clock cost** flagged for production planning; not a blocker for the one-time cutover sample but worth noting for any re-run.
