call_code_descriptions:
    - code: NLM
    description: Voicemail, not leave message - Voicemail encountered and there was no meaningful conversation with customer. The agent may provide their name and company information but DOES NOT mention the customer's name.
    - code: LM
    description: Voicemail encountered , left message - Voicemail was encountered and the agent mentions the customer's name, or contained appropriate identification information e.g. "May I please speak to [Customer Name]".
    - code: HUP
    description: Hang Up - Call was disconnected immediately after customer picked up, with no information exchange. The customer may have answered but remained silent or hung up before any meaningful conversation could take place. This represents a failed contact attempt where no account or personal data was verified.
    - code: IDF
    description: ID Verification Failed - Customer fails ID verification or refuses to verify their identity through standard verification protocols. This may occur when the customer cannot provide correct information for DOB, SSN, or zip code verification, or when they refuse to participate in the verification process entirely.
    - code: HUA
    description: Hang Up During Authentication - The customer confirmed their identity but disconnected during the formal ID verification process. The call ended while agent was attempting to verify personal information, such as date of birth, social security number, or zip code. This represents an incomplete verification attempt where the initial name confirmation was successful, but full verification was not completed.
    - code: NVR
    description: Name Verification Refused - Customer explicitly declined to confirm their full name, resulting in call termination. This may occur when the customer is suspicious of the call, concerned about privacy, or unwilling to engage with a collection effort. Without name confirmation, the conversation cannot legally proceed to account details.
    - code: IDV
    description: |
        ID Verification Successful - Customer successfully passed all required identity verification checks to confirm they are the intended recipient.
        The sucessful verification process must include 2 steps of confirmation: custmomer name and at least one of the following personal identifiers: date of birth, SSN, or address details.
    - code: TSR
    description: Transfer to Representative - Customer explicitly requested to speak with a human representative rather than continuing with the automated system. This request was acknowledged and confirmed, requiring the call to be routed to an available human agent. This transfer is tracked for quality assurance and staffing optimization purposes.
    - code: INC
    description: Consumer Incapacitated - Third party reports that the account holder is physically or mentally incapacitated and unable to handle their financial affairs. This status requires documentation and special handling procedures including potential contact with legal guardians or power of attorney representatives. The account may require special disposition based on the nature and permanence of the incapacitation.
    - code: LGB
    description: Language Barrier - Communication could not proceed effectively due to language differences between the caller and the customer. The customer indicated inability to understand or communicate in the language being used, making meaningful conversation impossible. This may require transfer to a bilingual agent or use of translation services for future contact attempts.
    - code: RTP
    description: Refusal to Pay - Customer explicitly declined to make any payment on the account, ONLY after the creditor made MULTIPLE attempts to negotiate a resolution. Throughout the conversation, the creditor provided multiple payment plan or settlement options, all of which the customer rejected. The customer acknowledged the debt and consistently confirmed their unwillingness or inability to pay despite repeated efforts to find a solution. This outcome must be well-documented to ensure compliance and to inform future contact strategies. If the customer passes all verification checks but declines to make payment on the first attempt, the call should still be classified as "ID Verification Failed" instead of "Refusal to Pay."
    - code: RCR
    description: Recording Consent Refused - Customer explicitly declined consent for the call to be recorded, requiring call termination. Recording consent is often required for compliance, quality assurance, and legal protection purposes in collection calls. Without this consent, company policy typically prevents proceeding with account discussion. If the customer does not agree to be recorded, prioritize the call classification accordingly.
    - code: NCV
    description: Not Convenient - Customer indicated that the current time is inappropriate or inconvenient for this discussion. The customer confirmed they are the correct person but stated they are unable to engage in a financial conversation at this moment due to timing, location, or other circumstantial factors. This requires scheduling an alternative contact time. MUST NOT select this code if the user claims to be busy (e.g., "I have a minute", "I'm in public") but STILL asks the agent to proceed (e.g., "Talk to me", "I'm interested", "Go ahead"). If the user stays on the line and engages in the conversation despite the distraction, the call is considered CONVENIENT enough to proceed.
    - code: CAB
    description: Callback Requested - Customer acknowledged the call but requested to be contacted at a specific future time that is more convenient for them. A specific callback date and time was established and confirmed with the customer for follow-up.
    - code: WCR
    description: Written Communication Only Requested - Customer has specifically requested that all future communications be conducted in writing only, with no phone calls. This preference was explicitly confirmed and must be honored according to consumer protection regulations. Future contact attempts must be limited to postal mail or other written communication methods as permitted by law.
    - code: CDS
    description: Cease and Desist - Customer has formally requested the complete cessation of all communication attempts regarding this account. This legal request was acknowledged and confirmed, requiring immediate flagging in the system to prevent any further contact. Violation of cease and desist requests can result in legal penalties under consumer protection laws.
    - code: CPR
    description: Complaint Received - Customer expressed dissatisfaction and formally registered a complaint about the account, collection practices, or other aspects of their experience. The nature of the complaint was documented but not fully resolved during the call. This requires escalation to appropriate departments for investigation and resolution according to consumer protection protocols.
    - code: HCH
    description: |
        Hardship Concern - Customer has indicated financial difficulty, inability to pay, or circumstances preventing payment. This includes but is not limited to: unemployment, reduced income, medical expenses, family emergencies, divorce, disability, business closure, or any other legitimate financial constraints that impact their ability to meet payment obligations. Collection activities should be adjusted to explore hardship options and payment arrangements appropriate to the customer's situation. Must NOT select this code if the customer mentions PAST struggles (like "I was on welfare") but explicitly states they have found a new job (e.g., "I just got hired", "got a job today"). Finding a job indicates the hardship is resolving, so this code does NOT apply.
    - code: PPA
    description: Payment Plan Agreed - Customer has agreed to a structured payment plan with specific terms for resolving the debt. The arrangement includes defined payment amounts, frequencies, and duration as mutually agreed upon during the negotiation. This plan has been formally accepted and the customer has been transferred to complete payment details. There is a special case to detect, if the customer is unable to respond at the time and requests to be contacted later, the same classification should apply. This also applies if the customer mentioned they have made a payment within the last 14 days via any channel, provided they supplied information about the payment date and amount. Do NOT select this code if the user says "yeah", "okay", or "sure" merely as filler words while distracted, rambling, or talking about unrelated topics (e.g., fireworks, personal stories). A PPA requires a clear, focused confirmation of the payment terms.
    - code: PPIF
    description: Promise to Pay in Full - Customer has agreed to pay the entire outstanding balance in a single payment. They have confirmed the full amount due and committed to a specific payment date, but have not yet completed the actual payment transaction. Follow-up will be required to ensure the promised payment is received as scheduled.
    - code: PSIF
    description: Promise to Settle in Full - Customer has accepted a discounted settlement offer that will resolve the account for less than the full balance. The settlement terms include a specific payment amount and timeline as agreed during negotiation, representing a mutually beneficial resolution. This agreement requires documentation and follow-up to ensure payment is received according to settlement terms.
    - code: WRN
    description: Wrong Number - The person who answered the call indicated that they are not the intended recipient and have no connection to the account holder. It appears that the number is incorrectly linked to the account and should be removed from active contact attempts. Immediate updating of the contact information in the system is necessary to prevent further misdirected calls. If the conversation includes inappropriate language and the caller has not successfully passed all required identity verification checks to confirm they are the intended recipient, classify the call with the code "Wrong Number."
    - code: TPC
    description: Third-Party Contact - A person other than the account holder provided new or updated contact information for reaching the intended recipient. While not authorized to discuss account details, this third party offered alternative ways to reach the actual account holder or suggested that they could pass along the message to the account holder. This new contact information must be verified before being used for future communication attempts. If the caller is not the intended recipient, classify the call accordingly.
    - code: FRA
    description: Fraud Reported - Customer claims the account was created fraudulently or through identity theft without their knowledge or consent. The customer has denied responsibility for the debt based on fraudulent activity and requested investigation. This allegation requires immediate escalation for fraud investigation and potential reporting to credit bureaus and authorities.
    - code: DNC
    description: Do Not Call - Customer has explicitly requested to be removed from all phone contact lists and to cease calling this specific phone number. This request must be honored immediately to comply with consumer protection regulations and company policies. The number should be flagged in the system to prevent future outbound calls. This differs from a complete cease and desist as it only applies to phone contact at this specific number, not all forms of communication. Proper documentation of this request is essential for compliance tracking.
    - code: DIS
    description: Debt Disputed - The customer has raised concerns regarding the accuracy, validity, or legitimacy of the debt. This includes disputes over the debt's ownership, amount, or previous payments not reflected in the current balance. Additionally, the customer may have identified errors in account information or requested formal validation documentation. This situation requires immediate account notation, suspension of collection activities, and investigation or validation in accordance with applicable regulations. However, this does NOT apply if the customer mentioned they have made a payment within the last 14 days via any channel, provided they supplied information about the payment date and amount.
    - code: CSM
    description: SCRA Coverage Reported - Customer verified active military service status which invokes special protections under the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act. The customer provided details about their military service including branch, status, and anticipated service period. SCRA protections include interest rate caps, foreclosure prevention, and other specific accommodations that must be immediately applied to the account.
    - code: PRS
    description: In Prison - Customer or third party confirmed that the account holder is currently incarcerated in a correctional facility. Details about the incarceration including facility location and expected release date may have been provided. This status requires special handling procedures and may affect collection strategies due to the customer's limited financial capabilities while imprisoned.
    - code: LGH
    description: Legal Guardian Handling - A legally authorized guardian is managing the account holder's affairs due to the account holder's incapacity or death. The guardian provided verifiable information about their legal relationship to the account holder and their authority to discuss the account. Communication about the account must now be directed exclusively to this authorized representative.
    - code: ATY
    description: Attorney Representation - Customer stated they have retained legal counsel to represent them regarding this debt, requiring all future communications be directed to their attorney. Select this call code if the caller or consumer lets you know that they are being represented by an attorney or lawyer (e.g. "I have an attorney" or "Speak to my lawyer" or "My lawyer told me not to talk to you. Please contact them instead")
    - code: BKP
    description: Bankruptcy - Customer reported current bankruptcy proceedings or intent to file bankruptcy that would include this account. The customer provided specific details about their bankruptcy filing including case number, chapter, and filing date. This legal status triggers immediate cessation of collection activities according to automatic stay provisions and requires verification with bankruptcy court records.
    - code: DSA
    description: |
        Debt Settlement Agreement - This code applies when a professional third-party entity (e.g., National Debt Relief, AmeriCor, or a law firm) is involved in managing the account. Select this code if The Consumer states they are enrolled in a program for debt/credit settlement, relief, consolidation, or financial management OR A Representative calls on behalf of the account holder to discuss settlement, negotiate terms, or make payments.
